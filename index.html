Origami is an art of folding paper.

Origami, as probably everyone knows, is the ancient Japanese art of paper folding. The name origami is derived from the Japanese words ‘ oru ‘ (to fold) and ‘ kami ‘ (paper). The name ‘origami’ was coined in 1880. Before that, the art was called ‘ orikata ‘ (folded shapes).

Some interesting facts about camels

  • There are mainly two species of true camels: the Dromedary and the Bactrian.
  • The Dromedary is found in the Middle East and is also called Arabian camel. They have a single hump on the back.
  • The Bactrian camel, also known as the Mongolian camel or domestic Bactrian camel, is a large even-toed ungulate native to the steppes of Central Asia. It has two humps on its back, in contrast to the single-humped dromedary camel.

Some interesting facts about chameleons

  • Chameleons are a very unique branch of the lizard group of reptiles. There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  • Chameleons live in warm varied habitats from rainforests through to deserts.
  • Almost half of the world's chameleon species are native to Madagascar, they are also found in Africa, and southern Europe to as far as Sri Lanka in Asia and have been introduced to Hawaii, California and Florida.

Some interesting facts about cicada

  • A cicada is a flying insect. Cicadas are members of the Cicadoidea superfamily.
  • There are about 3,000 known species of cicadas worldwide.
  • A cicada’s body color can be black, brown and green.

Some interesting facts about pandas

  • Giant pandas (often referred to as simply “pandas”) are black and white bears.
  • In the wild, they are found in thick bamboo forests, high up in the mountains of central China.
  • They spend up to 16 hours a day eating, just to gain enough sustenance for survival.

Some interesting facts about pigeons

  • One of the most fascinating things about pigeons is the variation in size, with the small ones weighing less than an ounce and the larger ones weighing nearly 10 pounds.
  • Pigeons also range in length from 5 inches to 19 inches.
  • The smaller ones aren’t necessarily babies and size is determined by many factors, genetics being one of the main ones.
